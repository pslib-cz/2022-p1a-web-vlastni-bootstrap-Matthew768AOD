Garden Snail (Helix aspersa)

This species of snail is one of the most common and well-known types of snail. It has a yellowish-brown shell with dark brown stripes and can grow up to about 1.3 inches (3.5 cm) in diameter. Garden snails are often considered pests in gardens and crops because they can cause damage to plants.

White Lipped Snail (Cepaea hortensis)

The white-lipped snail, scientific name Cepaea hortensis, is a species of air-breathing land snail that is native to Europe and is also found in parts of North Africa and Asia. It gets its name from the white or light-colored lip around the edge of its shell, which is usually brown or yellowish in color with dark stripes.

Ramshorn Snail (Planorbidae family)

This is a family of freshwater snails that can be found in ponds, lakes, and streams all over the world. They are called "ramshorn" snails because their shells are shaped like a ram's horn or coil. Some species of ramshorn snails are kept as pets in aquariums, where they are valued for their ability to help clean the tank.

Cone Snail (Conus species)

These are a group of venomous snails that are found in tropical waters around the world. They have a strikingly beautiful shell with intricate patterns and colors, but they are highly dangerous to humans. Some species of cone snail have venom that can cause paralysis or even death if they sting a person.

Japanese Land Snail (Euhadra brandtii)

This is a species of snail that is native to Japan and is often kept as a pet in other parts of the world. It has a round, brownish shell with distinctive spiraling ridges. Japanese land snails are popular pets because they are relatively easy to care for and can be bred in captivity.

Violet Sea Snail (Janthina janthina)

This species of snail is found in oceans all over the world and has a beautiful purple shell. Unlike most snails, the violet sea snail doesn't have a foot that it uses for crawling along surfaces. Instead, it floats on the surface of the water using a raft of bubbles that it creates with a special gland.
